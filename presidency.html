Major Accomplishments

As soon as Harding took office he instantly starting implementing his plan to achieve economic stability. One of his first accomplishments was that he made the Bureau of the Budget, which organized and managed government finances more effectively. He passed tax cuts that benefited businesses and wealthy people, with the plan to encourge investment. Harding’s administration also implemented protective tariffs to protect American industries from foreign competition, and he promoted policies that favored economic growth and business development.

Conflicts

Despite his efforts to recover the economy, Harding’s presidency is mainly known for the scandals that severely damaged his reputation. The most infamous of these scandals was the Teapot Dome scandal, in which Albert Fall the Secretary of the Interior at the time leased the federal oil reserves in Wyoming out to Harry Sinclair and Edward Doheny so they can drill for oil. Another scandal was, the Veterans Bureau scandal when millions of dollars were embezzeled by Charles R. Forbes. Although Harding was not personally involved in these scandals, they tarnished his presidency because he appointed all of the people who did these scandals and it created distrust in his leadership. Harding's reputation for honesty was severely damaged, and these scandals became a defining part of his legacy.

Legacy

The scandals aside, Harding’s presidency also had positive developments. He was a big advocate for civil rights, and he spoke out against lynching and supported federal anti-lynching legislation, but he was unable to achieve big legislative victory in this area. Harding’s leadership during his presidency was marked by a desire to bring stability to a nation recovering from war, but his time in office was cut short due to his sudden death from a heart attack on August 2, 1923. Harding’s death in office meant that he did not have the opportunity to have a second term leaving behind a legacy of both accomplishments and controversies.
